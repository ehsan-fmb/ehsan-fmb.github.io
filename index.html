Ehsan Futuhi

I am an AI researcher working on planning algorithms at the intersection of reinforcement learning, heuristic search, and foundation-model reasoning. I received my M.Sc. in Computing Science from the University of Alberta under the supervision of Prof. Martin Müller, where my research focused on improving the sample efficiency of policy-gradient algorithms in sparse-reward continuous control tasks through search and subgoal discovery.

I am currently a Data Scientist at MedWatch Technologies, where I work on AI methods for non-invasive blood glucose monitoring. I am also a part-time Research Assistant at the Moving AI Lab under the supervision of Prof. Nathan Sturtevant, where I study the planning and reasoning capabilities of large language models.
