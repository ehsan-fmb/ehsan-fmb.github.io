About

I am an AI researcher with an M.Sc. in Computing Science from the University of Alberta, where my research focused on reinforcement learning and search. I am currently working as a Data Scientist at MedWatch Technologies on non-invasive blood glucose monitoring using AI, and as a part-time Research Assistant at the Moving AI Lab, where I study the reasoning capabilities of large language models in planning tasks.

Broadly, I am interested in building intelligent systems that reason, plan, and generalize. My work connects classical search and planning algorithms with modern machine learning, including deep reinforcement learning, admissible heuristic learning, foundation-model reasoning, and efficient CPU–GPU implementations for large-scale search.

Research themes

Learning admissible heuristics

Designing learning objectives and theoretical tools for heuristic functions that support efficient and reliable A* search.

LLM reasoning for planning

Evaluating and improving how large language models solve structured planning problems through prompting and fine-tuning.

Batch heuristic search

Developing parallel CPU–GPU frameworks for batching heuristic operations in depth-first heuristic search.

Sparse-reward reinforcement learning

Improving exploration, replay, and learning efficiency in sparse-reward continuous-control environments.
